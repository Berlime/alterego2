## WordPress Campus Connect UTM Johor Bahru

Q1 peaked where it always feels best for me, inside a WordPress room.

[WordPress Campus Connect UTM 2026](https://events.wordpress.org/campusconnect/2026/universiti-teknologi-malaysia-johorbahru/) was the first Campus Connect in Malaysia. It landed on 3 January 2026 at Universiti Teknologi Malaysia, Johor Bahru. Students, organizers, and folks from the industry packed into P19, Dewan Kuliah 6, and the energy felt like a proper kickoff for the year.

![First Campus Connect in Malaysia at UTM Johor Bahru, 3 January 2026](cc.avif)

I wore a few hats again, organizer, speaker, and A/V. Same rhythm as [2025](/posts/2025) when I helped with Campus Connect as speaker lead and AV, so the crew already knew how we run sessions and who grabs the mic when something breaks. The [WP Meet Up Johor Bahru](/posts/wp-meet-up) crowd showed up in spirit too, lots of familiar faces from the local community.

![Organizer, speaker, and A/V at Campus Connect](cc2.avif)

My session was [The Must-Haves of Creating Your First WordPress Website](https://events.wordpress.org/campusconnect/2026/universiti-teknologi-malaysia-johorbahru/session/the-must-haves-of-creating-your-first-wordpress-website/). Domains, hosting, and the boring infrastructure decisions that save you pain later. Beginner friendly on purpose. You can read the [speaker bio](https://events.wordpress.org/campusconnect/2026/universiti-teknologi-malaysia-johorbahru/speaker/kamil-sharip/) if you want the formal version of who I am on stage.

Ending Q1 with that talk felt right. WordPress community in Johor Bahru, then straight into shipping things for clients.

## Monday.com Training, Penang

Early in the quarter I spent three days in Penang running monday.com training for Inari Amertron, a semiconductor company. The room was mostly HODs and engineers, not the marketing crowd I usually get in workshops, so the pace had to be tighter and the examples had to land on real workflows, not slide fluff.

I was the trainer for all three days. Same program each round, three different batches so HODs and engineers could join without one overloaded room.

Showing up as the trainer, not the builder or the speaker slot, was new for me. Engineers ask direct questions, so you know quickly what lands. By the third batch I was less nervous about owning the room. Good stretch for to end the first quarter of 2026.

## Berlime Wiki (Q1)

The rest of Q1 was mostly writing things down on the [Berlime wiki](https://wiki.berlime.com) so I could stop re-explaining the same stack in DMs. The main site at [berlime.com](https://berlime.com) still handles leads and the blog, the wiki is where packages and process live now.

### Shopify

I packaged what I learned from my own WooCommerce detour in [2025](/posts/2025) into a proper offering. Some clients outgrew marketplace selling but do not need a custom cart. We build in a dev store, transfer when they are happy, they pay on their own card from there. Tiers are on the [Shopify wiki page](https://wiki.berlime.com/website-packages/shopify-websites).

### Static Sites

Static was the obvious next one. Client work and this blog kept landing on static generators anyway, [Alter Ego 2](/posts/alter-ego-2) runs on [Hugo](/posts/hugo) right here. We gave it a [starter page on the wiki](https://wiki.berlime.com/website-packages/starter-websites) for when a site does not need WordPress along for the ride.

### AI for Teams

AI for Teams came back in January, before any of this. Teams kept paying for scattered ChatGPT seats with no shared visibility. We sketched self-hosted team chat, dogfooded it a bit, then wrote it up as [AI for Teams](https://wiki.berlime.com/other-services/ai-chats-for-teams).
